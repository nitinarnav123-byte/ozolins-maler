# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Homeowners and property owners in and around Moss, Norway, looking for a painter for interior/exterior painting, wallpapering, or plastering work. They often skew older, are evaluating local tradespeople, and want to quickly judge trustworthiness and craft quality before requesting a free site visit (befaring). They're not browsing for inspiration — they're deciding whether to call.

## Product Purpose

A single-page marketing and booking site for Ozolins Maler (founder Vairis Ozolins), a painting contractor based in Moss. It presents services, social proof, the founder's story, a gallery of work, and a booking/contact flow. Success is a visitor trusting the craftsmanship enough to request a free inspection within 24 hours.

## Brand Personality

A blend of three things that need to coexist: **warm craftsman** (personal, family-business trust), **premium/refined** (quality materials and finish, not a bargain-bin painter), and **no-nonsense local pro** (clear pricing, fast response, no fuss). In short: a skilled tradesman who takes pride in the work and treats your home like his own — refined taste, straightforward manner.

Three-word personality: **warm, refined, dependable**.

## Anti-references

- Generic tradesman-site clichés: stock blue/orange gradients, generic toolbox/paint-roller icon grids, stock photography that doesn't match the actual work.
- Generic AI marketing tropes: glassmorphism, neon/purple gradients, gradient text, hero-metric stat blocks, identical feature-card grids, tiny tracked eyebrows over every section.
- Anything that feels like a templated "local business website builder" output — the current editorial cream/navy/terracotta direction with serif display type is the right register and should be preserved/refined, not replaced.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust at a glance.** Visual quality itself should signal craftsmanship — the site is the first impression of the work quality.
2. **Editorial warmth over corporate gloss.** Lean into the serif display type, warm cream/sand/navy palette, and generous spacing rather than generic SaaS polish.
3. **Frictionless path to booking.** Every page should make "request a free inspection" feel like the obvious, easy next step.
4. **Readable for an older audience.** Prioritize clear hierarchy, generous type size, and strong contrast over decorative density.
5. **Show the real work.** Gallery, testimonials, and founder story carry credibility — don't bury them under decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Baseline: WCAG 2.1 AA, with extra weight on readability for an older audience:
- Favor larger body text and generous line-height over compact, decorative type.
- Strong color contrast (verify, don't eyeball) — especially for body copy and form labels.
- Large, clearly labeled tap targets for buttons and form fields.
- All interactive elements keyboard-navigable with visible focus states.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for scroll-reveal and other animations.
- Norwegian-language content stays clear and jargon-free.
